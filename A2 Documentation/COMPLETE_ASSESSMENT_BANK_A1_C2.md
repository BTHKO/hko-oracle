## 1. ASSESSMENT PHILOSOPHY & PRINCIPLES

### 1.1 Core Principles

**Alignment:**
- Every assessment directly measures stated objectives
- Tasks mirror real HR professional contexts
- Difficulty matches CEFR level descriptors

**Authenticity:**
- Real-world HR scenarios (not contrived textbook situations)
- Authentic materials (actual job postings, emails, contracts)
- Professional outcomes (hiring decisions, policy explanations)

**Fairness:**
- Clear criteria communicated before assessment
- Multiple attempts on formative assessments
- Accommodations for disabilities
- Bias-free language and contexts

**Actionability:**
- Feedback identifies specific strengths/weaknesses
- Recommendations for improvement provided
- Resources linked to remediation needs

### 1.2 Assessment Types

**Formative (40% of grade):**
- Purpose: Monitor progress, guide learning
- Frequency: Continuous throughout course
- Retakes: Unlimited (best score counts)
- Feedback: Automated + tutor comments

**Summative (60% of grade):**
- Purpose: Certify achievement of objectives
- Frequency: End of each module + final exam
- Retakes: 1 allowed if <60%
- Feedback: Detailed rubric + improvement plan

### 1.3 Four Skills Framework

Every assessment covers:
- **Listening:** Comprehend spoken HR communication
- **Reading:** Understand written HR documents
- **Speaking:** Produce oral HR communication
- **Writing:** Create written HR documents

Weighting per skill:
- A1-A2: Receptive skills (L+R) 55%, Productive skills (S+W) 45%
- B1-B2: Equal 50/50 split
- C1-C2: Productive skills (S+W) 55%, Receptive skills (L+R) 45%

## 2. RUBRICS BY SKILL

### 2.1 SPEAKING RUBRIC (All Levels)

**Categories:** Fluency, Accuracy, Pronunciation, Task Completion, Interaction

**10-Point Scale per Category (Total: 50 points)**

---

#### **FLUENCY (10 points)**

**A1 Level:**
- **9-10:** Speaks with some hesitation but completes simple phrases. Natural pauses for word search.
- **7-8:** Frequent pauses but message is clear. Simple sentences produced.
- **5-6:** Very slow with long pauses. Single words or very short phrases.
- **3-4:** Extreme difficulty; mostly isolated words. Cannot maintain flow.
- **1-2:** Minimal production; cannot form coherent phrases.

**A2 Level:**
- **9-10:** Speaks with noticeable pauses but can maintain simple descriptions/narratives.
- **7-8:** Hesitant but can produce connected sentences. Some false starts.
- **5-6:** Frequent pauses; struggles to connect ideas smoothly.
- **3-4:** Very choppy; difficulty with sentence-level production.
- **1-2:** Barely comprehensible; word-level only.

**B1 Level:**
- **9-10:** Speaks with reasonable fluency on familiar topics. Minor hesitations.
- **7-8:** Some hesitation but maintains conversation. Occasional rephrasing.
- **5-6:** Noticeable pauses when searching for expressions or structures.
- **3-4:** Frequent breakdowns; struggles with extended discourse.
- **1-2:** Cannot maintain extended speech; phrase-level only.

**B2 Level:**
- **9-10:** Speaks fluently and spontaneously. Natural interaction without strain.
- **7-8:** Generally fluent; occasional pausing for complex ideas.
- **5-6:** Some hesitation on abstract topics; mostly fluent on concrete topics.
- **3-4:** Fluency breaks down on complex topics; struggles with elaboration.
- **1-2:** Limited fluency even on familiar topics.

**C1 Level:**
- **9-10:** Fluent, spontaneous expression. Effortless even on complex topics.
- **7-8:** Very fluent; minor pauses for precise word choice.
- **5-6:** Generally fluent but occasional hesitation on specialized HR topics.
- **3-4:** Fluency adequate for routine topics but breaks on advanced concepts.
- **1-2:** Noticeable lack of fluency; struggles with professional register.

**C2 Level:**
- **9-10:** Effortlessly fluent; native-like spontaneity and precision.
- **7-8:** Extremely fluent; seamless even on nuanced HR topics.
- **5-6:** Fluent but may pause for extremely precise expressions.
- **3-4:** Adequate fluency but lacks native-like ease.
- **1-2:** Noticeably non-native; some hesitation.

---

#### **ACCURACY (10 points)**

**A1 Level:**
- **9-10:** Basic structures mostly correct (to be, have, present simple). Errors don't impede.
- **7-8:** Some errors in basic structures but meaning clear.
- **5-6:** Frequent errors but can produce simple correct sentences.
- **3-4:** Many errors; only occasional correct structures.
- **1-2:** Grammar almost always incorrect; meaning obscured.

**A2 Level:**
- **9-10:** Uses past/future accurately in simple contexts. Minor errors acceptable.
- **7-8:** Generally accurate with common structures; some mistakes with complex forms.
- **5-6:** Errors with verb tenses but basic structures sound.
- **3-4:** Frequent grammatical errors affecting clarity.
- **1-2:** Accuracy too low for A2; mostly A1 structures with errors.

**B1 Level:**
- **9-10:** Good control of common structures. Errors don't cause misunderstanding.
- **7-8:** Reasonably accurate but errors with complex grammar (perfect tenses, conditionals).
- **5-6:** Adequate accuracy for routine topics; errors increase with complexity.
- **3-4:** Many errors; some interfere with comprehension.
- **1-2:** Insufficient accuracy for B1; mostly A2 level.

**B2 Level:**
- **9-10:** Consistent accuracy even with complex structures. Occasional slips.
- **7-8:** Good accuracy; errors mainly in low-frequency structures.
- **5-6:** Generally accurate but errors with advanced grammar (passives, reported speech).
- **3-4:** Accuracy breaks down on complex topics.
- **1-2:** Too many errors for B2 level.

**C1 Level:**
- **9-10:** High degree of accuracy. Rare errors, self-corrects.
- **7-8:** Very accurate; minor errors only.
- **5-6:** Good accuracy but occasional mistakes with very advanced structures.
- **3-4:** Accuracy adequate for B2 but not C1.
- **1-2:** Insufficient accuracy; multiple noticeable errors.

**C2 Level:**
- **9-10:** Near-perfect accuracy. Errors extremely rare.
- **7-8:** Excellent accuracy; virtually error-free.
- **5-6:** Very high accuracy with occasional minor slips.
- **3-4:** Good accuracy but not at mastery level.
- **1-2:** Noticeable errors; not C2 level.

---

#### **PRONUNCIATION (10 points)**

**All Levels:**
- **9-10:** Clear, easily understood. Accent does not impede comprehension.
- **7-8:** Generally clear; minor pronunciation issues don't affect meaning.
- **5-6:** Understandable but accent affects clarity. Some sounds mispronounced.
- **3-4:** Difficult to understand; significant pronunciation problems.
- **1-2:** Nearly incomprehensible due to pronunciation.

**Spanish Speaker Specific Issues to Listen For:**
- /b/ vs /v/ distinction (very/berry)
- /dʒ/ sound (job, judge) often pronounced /y/
- /ʃ/ vs /tʃ/ (she/cheat)
- Final consonants dropped (work → "wor")
- -ed endings (worked → "work", not "worked")
- Vowel length (ship/sheep, live/leave)

---

**A1 Level:**
- **9-10:** Completes task with all required info (name, job, company). Simple but complete.
- **7-8:** Most info included; minor omissions don't affect task success.
- **5-6:** Some required info missing but attempts all parts.
- **3-4:** Incomplete; misses major elements of task.
- **1-2:** Minimal attempt; task not addressed.

**A2 Level:**

- **7-8:** Adequate description; most elements present.
- **5-6:** Basic task completion but lacks detail.
- **3-4:** Incomplete; missing key elements.
- **1-2:** Task barely attempted.

**B1 Level:**
- **9-10:** Expresses opinion with reasons. Clear position on HR topic.
- **7-8:** Opinion expressed; most supporting points included.
- **5-6:** States opinion but limited justification.
- **3-4:** Opinion unclear or unsupported.
- **1-2:** Does not complete task.

**B2 Level:**
- **9-10:** Develops argument coherently. Presents multiple perspectives on HR issue.
- **7-8:** Good argumentation; most points well-developed.
- **5-6:** Basic argument but lacks depth or counterarguments.
- **3-4:** Weak argumentation; superficial treatment.
- **1-2:** Task requirements not met.

**C1 Level:**
- **9-10:** Sophisticated treatment of complex HR topic. Nuanced analysis.
- **7-8:** Strong analysis; most aspects well-covered.
- **5-6:** Adequate but lacks sophistication expected at C1.
- **3-4:** Treatment too simple for C1 level.
- **1-2:** Does not meet C1 task demands.

**C2 Level:**
- **9-10:** Masterful handling of topic. Executive-level discourse.
- **7-8:** Excellent treatment; near-native proficiency evident.
- **5-6:** Very good but minor gaps in completeness.
- **3-4:** Good but not at mastery level.
- **1-2:** Does not demonstrate C2 competence.

---

#### **INTERACTION (10 points)** *(For dialogues/interviews only)*

**All Levels:**
- **9-10:** Natural turn-taking. Responds appropriately. Asks relevant questions.
- **7-8:** Good interaction; mostly natural exchanges.
- **5-6:** Adequate interaction but some awkwardness.
- **3-4:** Limited interaction; struggles with turn-taking.
- **1-2:** Minimal interaction; mostly one-way.

**For Monologues:** Replace with **ORGANIZATION (10 points)**
- **9-10:** Clear structure (intro, body, conclusion). Logical flow.
- **7-8:** Good organization; mostly coherent.
- **5-6:** Basic organization but some confusion.
- **3-4:** Poorly organized; hard to follow.
- **1-2:** Disorganized; no clear structure.

### 2.3 LISTENING RUBRIC (Comprehension Assessment)

**Not rubric-based; uses multiple-choice, true/false, short answer questions**

**Scoring:**
- Each question worth 1-2 points
- Total: 25-30 points per listening assessment
- Convert to percentage

**Question Types by Level:**

**A1:**
- Multiple choice (3 options, simple)
- True/False
- Match speaker to statement
- Fill in numbers/names/dates

**A2:**
- Multiple choice (4 options)
- True/False/Not Given
- Note completion
- Multiple matching

**B1:**
- Multiple choice (complex options)
- Sentence completion
- Summary completion
- Multiple matching (6+ items)

**B2-C2:**
- Multiple choice (similar-sounding options)
- Note/summary completion (paraphrased)
- Multiple matching (nuanced distinctions)
- Inference questions (What does speaker imply?)

---

### 2.4 READING RUBRIC (Comprehension Assessment)

**Similar to Listening - question-based scoring**

**Question Types by Level:**

**A1:**
- Multiple choice (simple vocab)
- True/False
- Match heading to paragraph
- Find specific info (scan)

**A2:**
- Multiple choice
- True/False/Not Stated
- Match information
- Identify main idea

**B1:**
- Multiple choice (paraphrased options)
- Matching headings
- Identify writer's opinion/purpose
- Inference questions

**B2-C2:**
- Multiple choice (subtle differences)
- Match opinions to people
- Identify writer's attitude/tone
- Complex inference
- Recognize organization/structure

## 3. QUESTION TEMPLATES BY TYPE

### 3.1 LISTENING QUESTION TEMPLATES

#### **Template 1: Multiple Choice (All Levels)**

**Format:**
```
Listen to [context]. Answer questions 1-5.

1. What is the speaker's main point?
   A) [Option - partially correct]
   B) [Option - correct]
   C) [Option - distractor with similar vocabulary]
   D) [Option - clearly incorrect] [B1+]

Key: B
```

**A1 Example:**
```
Listen to Maria introduce herself. Answer questions 1-3.

1. What is Maria's job?
   A) Manager
   B) Recruiter
   C) Assistant

Key: B
```

**C1 Example:**
```
Listen to the VP of HR discuss restructuring. Answer 1-6.

1. What is the speaker's primary concern regarding the proposed restructuring?
   A) The timeline may be too aggressive given union negotiations
   B) The cost savings projections seem overly optimistic
   C) The communication strategy hasn't been fully developed
   D) The impact on employee morale hasn't been adequately addressed

Key: C
```

---

#### **Template 2: Note Completion (B1+)**

**Format:**
```
Complete the notes below. Write NO MORE THAN TWO WORDS for each answer.

TOPIC: [Title]
Main point: ___________
Supporting detail 1: ___________
Supporting detail 2: ___________
Conclusion: ___________
```

**B2 Example:**
```
Complete the notes about the new benefits package.

EMPLOYEE BENEFITS UPDATE
Effective date: January 1st
Health insurance provider: ___________ (1)
Dental coverage: Now includes ___________ (2)
Retirement match: Increased to ___________ (3)%

Keys: (1) Blue Cross (2) orthodontics (3) 6
```

---

#### **Template 3: True/False/Not Given (A2+)**

**Format:**
```
Do the following statements agree with the information in the recording?
Write:
TRUE if the statement agrees with the information
FALSE if the statement contradicts the information
NOT GIVEN if there is no information on this

1. [Statement that is clearly true]
2. [Statement that is clearly false]
3. [Statement not mentioned in recording]
```

**A2 Example:**
```
Listen to the job interview. True/False/Not Given.

1. The candidate has 5 years of experience. TRUE
2. She speaks three languages. FALSE (she speaks two)
3. She graduated from university in 2018. NOT GIVEN
```

### 3.2 READING QUESTION TEMPLATES

#### **Template 4: Matching Headings (B1+)**

**Format:**
```
The reading passage has [X] paragraphs A-E.
Choose the correct heading for each paragraph from the list below.

Headings:
i. [Heading]
ii. [Heading]
iii. [Heading]
iv. [Heading]
v. [Heading]
vi. [Heading - extra distractor]
vii. [Heading - extra distractor]

Paragraph A: ___
Paragraph B: ___
[etc.]
```

**B1 Example:**
```
Choose the correct heading for paragraphs A-D.

Headings:
i. Benefits of diverse hiring
ii. Challenges in recruitment
iii. The role of technology
iv. Training new employees
v. Legal compliance issues [extra]
vi. Budget considerations [extra]

Paragraph A (discusses recruitment difficulties): ii
Paragraph B (discusses onboarding process): iv
Paragraph C (discusses AI in hiring): iii
Paragraph D (discusses why diversity matters): i
```

---

#### **Template 5: Summary Completion (B2+)**

**Format:**
```
Complete the summary using words from the box.

[Box with 10-12 words including distractors]

The article discusses _____(1)_____. According to the author, _____(2)_____ is the main challenge. The solution proposed involves _____(3)_____.

Keys: (1) [word] (2) [word] (3) [word]
```
