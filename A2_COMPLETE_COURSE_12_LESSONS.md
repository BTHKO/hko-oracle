### 6. UNDERSTANDING THE GRAMMAR: PAST SIMPLE (REGULAR VERBS)

**Grammar Table:**

| | Affirmative | Negative | Question |
|---|-------------|----------|----------|
| **I / You** | worked | did not work<br>didn't work | Did I/you work? |
| **He / She / It** | worked | did not work<br>didn't work | Did he/she/it work? |
| **We / They** | worked | did not work<br>didn't work | Did we/they work? |

**Spanish (A2):** Usamos Past Simple para acciones terminadas en el pasado.

**Key Rules:**

1. **Formation:** Base verb + **-ed**
   - start → start**ed**
   - work → work**ed**
   - apply → appl**ied** (y → ied after consonant)
   - stop → stopp**ed** (double consonant after short vowel)

2. **Use:** Completed actions in the past with specific time
   - I started in 2018. ✓
   - I started yesterday. ✓
   - I started (general past). ✓

3. **Time Expressions:**
   - **Specific:** in 2018, last year, yesterday, two days ago, in March
   - **Sequence:** first, then, after that, finally

4. **Negatives & Questions:** Use DID (not -ed)
   - ❌ I didn't worked
   - ✓ I didn't work
   - ❌ Did you worked?
   - ✓ Did you work?

### 16. QUESTIONS ABOUT THE PAST

**Grammar Table: Past Simple Questions**

| Question Type | Structure | Example | Short Answer |
|---------------|-----------|---------|--------------|
| **Yes/No** | Did + S + base verb? | Did you work in HR? | Yes, I did. / No, I didn't. |
| **WH-Question** | WH + did + S + base verb? | When did you start? | In 2020. |
| **Subject Question** | Who/What + past verb? | Who started yesterday? | Maria started yesterday. |

**Key Rules:**

1. **Yes/No Questions:** Did + subject + BASE VERB (not -ed)
   - ✓ Did you work in payroll?
   - ❌ Did you worked in payroll?

2. **WH-Questions:** Question word + did + subject + BASE VERB
   - ✓ Where did you work?
   - ❌ Where you worked?
   - ❌ Where did you worked?

3. **Subject Questions:** No "did" needed (who/what is subject)
   - ✓ Who started the project? (Maria started it)
   - ✓ What happened yesterday? (The meeting happened)

**Spanish (A2):** En preguntas, usamos DID + verbo base (sin -ed).

### 6. PAST SIMPLE NEGATIVE: THE RULE

**Grammar Table:**

| Subject | didn't | Base Verb | Example |
|---------|--------|-----------|---------|
| I | didn't | work | I didn't work yesterday. |
| You | didn't | apply | You didn't apply on time. |
| He/She/It | didn't | qualify | She didn't qualify. |
| We | didn't | expect | We didn't expect this. |
| They | didn't | attend | They didn't attend. |

**Key Rules:**

1. **Formation:** didn't + BASE VERB (never -ed)
   - ✓ I didn't work
   - ❌ I didn't worked

2. **All Subjects Same:** No changes for he/she/it
   - ✓ He didn't work
   - ✓ She didn't work
   - ✓ It didn't work

3. **Full Form:** did not = didn't
   - "I did not expect" (formal)
   - "I didn't expect" (common)

**Spanish (A2):** Para negativo, usa didn't + verbo base (sin -ed).

# LESSON 12: "WE'VE JUST HIRED THREE PEOPLE"
**Grammar:** Present Perfect (just/already/yet) | **Theme:** Recent Results

## VOCABULARY (8 words)
hired, promoted, transferred, onboarded, trained, completed, finished, achieved

**Audio:** "Recent HR Achievements" - Team update: "Good news! We've just hired three new employees. We've already onboarded two of them. We haven't trained the third person yet. We've successfully filled all open positions this month..."

**Grammar:**
```
JUST: very recent (happened a short time ago)
"We've just hired someone" (today/this morning)

ALREADY: completed sooner than expected
"I've already reviewed all CVs" (finished!)

YET: not happened but expected (negatives/questions)
"Have you contacted them yet?" | "I haven't called yet"
```

**Position:**
- JUST/ALREADY: between have/has and past participle
  - We've JUST hired...
  - She's ALREADY started...
- YET: at end of sentence
  - Have you called yet?
  - I haven't finished yet.

**Typical HR Updates:**
- We've just posted the job opening
- She's already accepted the offer
- Have you interviewed the candidates yet?
- We haven't made a decision yet
