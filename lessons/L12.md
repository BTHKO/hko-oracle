# LESSON 12: "WE'VE JUST HIRED THREE PEOPLE"
**Grammar:** Present Perfect (just/already/yet) | **Theme:** Recent Results

## VOCABULARY (8 words)
hired, promoted, transferred, onboarded, trained, completed, finished, achieved

**Audio:** "Recent HR Achievements" - Team update: "Good news! We've just hired three new employees. We've already onboarded two of them. We haven't trained the third person yet. We've successfully filled all open positions this month..."

**Grammar:**
```
JUST: very recent (happened a short time ago)
"We've just hired someone" (today/this morning)

ALREADY: completed sooner than expected
"I've already reviewed all CVs" (finished!)

YET: not happened but expected (negatives/questions)
"Have you contacted them yet?" | "I haven't called yet"
```

**Position:**
- JUST/ALREADY: between have/has and past participle
  - We've JUST hired...
  - She's ALREADY started...
- YET: at end of sentence
  - Have you called yet?
  - I haven't finished yet.

**Typical HR Updates:**
- We've just posted the job opening
- She's already accepted the offer
- Have you interviewed the candidates yet?
- We haven't made a decision yet
